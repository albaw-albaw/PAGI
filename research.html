Our R&D portfolio

Spanning pavement engineering, renewable energy, AI monitoring, and electromobility across three levels: System & Policy, Innovation, and Technical Solution.

Electrified Roadway · WPT · ECC
Electromobility

Electrified Roadway Design with Wireless Power Transfer for EVs

Precast Ultra-thin Whitetopping (PUTW) using ECC for rapid conversion of existing roads into EV-charging corridors, eliminating steel reinforcement interference with inductive charging fields.

Partners: TUM · Nanyang Technological University
ECC · Skid Resistance · BPN >90
Advanced Materials

Structural & Functional Performance of ECC Pavement

Corundum-integrated ECC achieving BPN >90 with grooved surface treatment. Durability validated over 100,000 load cycles with supplementary cementitious materials including fly ash and slag.

Partners: TUM · Nanyang Technological University
Solar PV · MRT · 18 MWp
Energy Harvesting

Solar PV on Ballasted & Ballastless Railway Tracks

PV system for Singapore's MRT leveraging 200,000 m² of track area — ≥18 MWp capacity, 22.56 GWh/year production, saving 261,600+ tonnes of CO₂/year.

Partners: TUM · NUS SERIS
PVNB · 157.6 GWh/yr · Noise
Multi-functional Infrastructure

Photovoltaics Noise Barrier (PVNB) on Railway Tracks

T-shaped barrier combining acoustic mitigation and solar generation producing ~157.6 GWh/year — powering the equivalent of 445,323 Singapore four-room public housing flats.

Partners: TUM · NUS SERIS
AIoT · PCI · >85% accuracy
AI / Digital Infrastructure

Digital Pavement Intelligence Optimization System

Scalable AIoT pavement monitoring at 30–60 km/h with >85% accuracy. IoT sensors + ML enable predictive preservation management at a fraction of conventional LIDAR system costs.

Partners: TUM
WPT · ECC · +9.1% capacity
Logistics · Electromobility

Electrified Aisle for E-Forklift Warehouse Operations

ECC floor with embedded WPT enables continuous e-forklift operation. Slabs 31% thinner than concrete; +9.1% material handling capacity at ~50% lower lifecycle cost with significant CO₂ savings.

Partners: TUM
CFD · ANSYS Fluent · Drainage
Fluid Dynamics

CFD 3D Model on Surface Drainage Pavement Performance

ANSYS Fluent model simulating rainfall-loading on Singapore highways to optimize pavement surface geometry. Validated within 5.6% of lab results and 3.4% of analytical equations.

Partners: TUM
TiO₂ · Photocatalysis · Tunnel
Photocatalysis · Tunnels

Self-Cleaning Photocatalysis ECC Pavement for Tunnels

Carbon-modified TiO₂ ECC with visible-light photocatalysis. Compressive strength >50 MPa, BPN ~80, lightness value 90 — recovering 90% reflectance after just 4 hours of visible light exposure.

Partners: TUM · Nanyang Technological University
AI · mAP50 0.863 · 7000+ images
AI · Pavement Monitoring

AI-Based Monitoring & Analysis of Highway Pavement Cracks

ML model trained on 7,000+ images detecting longitudinal, transversal, and alligator cracks with mAP50 of 0.863, enabling automated Pavement Condition Index assessment and preservation planning.

Partners: TUM · IIUM
